What is DECK#?

DECK# is a layered annotation protocol for a standard playing card deck (52 + 2 Jokers). Its purpose is to allow anyone to build and play complex board games using materials available anywhere, at minimal cost: a standard French-suited deck and two permanent markers.

Inspired by Piecepack (James Kyle, 2000), DECK# is an open system that defines a shared graphic vocabulary — 7 geometric glyphs, spatial zones, and connectivity markers — without prescribing game mechanics or semantics. Games built on DECK# assign their own meanings to this vocabulary.

DECK# is language-agnostic: the entire graphic vocabulary consists of geometric glyphs and Arabic numerals. No text is necessary or permitted in annotations.

7 primary glyphs — • + × △ ○ □ Fixed vocabulary, open semantics.

2 markers only Black and red permanent ink. Color is derived, never a free choice.

Two profiles Base (hand-held cards) and Extended (grid/tactical tiles).
